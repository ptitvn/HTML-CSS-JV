Taylor Swift

Taylor Alison Swift (born December 13, 1989) is an American singer-songwriter. Known for her narrative songwriting, artistic reinventions, and cultural influence, Swift is a leading figure in popular music and is the subject of widespread media coverage, with a large fan base known as Swifties.

Swift signed a record deal with Big Machine Records in 2005 and debuted as a country singer with the albums 'Taylor Swift' (2006) and 'Fearless' (2008). Singles like 'Teardrops on My Guitar,' 'Love Story,' and 'You Belong with Me' achieved success in both country and pop music formats. She incorporated rock in 'Speak Now' (2010) and electronic elements in 'Red' (2012), then transitioned from country to pop with her synth-pop album '1989' (2014); media scrutiny inspired her hip-hop influenced album 'Reputation' (2017). These albums produced Billboard Hot 100 number-one singles such as 'We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together,' 'Shake It Off,' 'Blank Space,' 'Bad Blood,' and 'Look What You Made Me Do".
